HOUR OF CODE

The Hour of Code started as a one-hour introduction to computer science, designed to demystify "code", to show that anybody can learn the basics, and to broaden participation in the field of computer science. It has since become a worldwide effort to celebrate computer science, starting with 1-hour coding activities but expanding to all sorts of community efforts.

Hour of Code

The Hour of Code is an annual global event that people are taught straightforwardly the fundamentals of computer programming in 1 hour in an entertaining way. This event is part of a nonprofit organization initiated in the United States by Code.org,  to incorporate the importance of technical skill in the US curriculum and increase awareness about coding. In addition, with the Hour of Code, it is more productive to discover highly technical children, to facilitate the use of digital devices by technophobic children, especially those studying in remote rural areas who are unable to use technology due to their financial situation, and to help contribute to the future of technology by increasing interest in areas such as the School of Information and Technology. It is a notable result that with the organization of this event in countries around the world, the interest of girls in the field of SITE and research is growing. Several of these events have been held in Azerbaijan, one of which was held in December with the support of ADA University, led by Nabat Gaimzada, Iltun Huseynli, Farid Mehdiyev, and Nijat Shirinov.


Short Report from Our Hour of Code Activity

Our "Hour of Code" project was started in the. First of all, we have to choose a school for our project. After that, we decided to choose the school of "District of Zangilan – number of 19th schools" which is established in Sumgait. Our group started meeting at the digital platform of "Zoom" at the beginning of preparing. Choosing 7th grades for a teaching game called “Classic Maze” which is appropriate for 12-13 years old children was decided. Our activity day was planned to start 12th of December at 9.30 in the meeting zoom. They were approximately 23 participants in an online meeting. Our group member Nijat Shirinov started to talk first about ADA University. Then, Nabat Gasimzade started to give questions to test children’s information about programming. Afterward, Farid Mehdiyev started tutoring part of programming. He talked about movements and limitations in the “Classic Maze”. After Farid finished, Iltun Huseynov continues to talk the game.
