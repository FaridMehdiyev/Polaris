In the classroom environment, robots foster problem solving, innovative thinking, and a positive feeling of competitiveness that fuels student creativity. The lack of access to the tools needed to create and program robots in educational institutions negatively affects students' ability to develop their technical skills and interest in robotic projects.These discourage prevent many students from entering the robotic world although the basic principles of robotics are explained in certain lessons approved by the Ministries of Education. However it is noticeable that In the 21st century, these difficulties are being overcome with the US-Danish toy company Lego and its product Lego Mindstorms.


Our Team Robotic Project

Due to the pandemic, education in the universities continues to be conducted online. For this reason, there are a number of assignments at ADA University, especially those based on problem-solving skills that can be prepared through the Internet and technology. One of them is the "Robotic Project". Since the device required for the task is not available, this task will be solved by us as "programmers living in previous years". More clearly, earlier programmers coded programs that were completely imaginary. They made a plan and estimated its working principle without any technological assistance. Now we have the opportunity to test our thinking skills and travel to the "ancient programmer's life."

Since we do not have the opportunity to meet, we share and hold our plans and meetings online. Also, the "Zoom" online platform has been our assistant during online meetings.
